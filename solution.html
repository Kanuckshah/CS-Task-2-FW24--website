E-Waste Regulations Around the World

Governments and organizations have introduced several policies to address the growing e-waste problem. In the European Union, the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) directive holds manufacturers responsible for the safe disposal and recycling of their electronics. Similarly, Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) laws require tech companies to ensure their products are recycled properly.

Energy Efficiency Standards

Policies such as the ENERGY STAR certification help ensure that computers and other electronic devices are designed to be energy-efficient. By using these certified devices, users can significantly reduce their energy consumption, which in turn lowers the carbon footprint of computer usage.

Corporate Initiatives

Tech giants like Apple, Dell, and HP have established take-back programs that allow customers to return their old devices for proper recycling. Apple, for instance, uses a robot named Daisy to disassemble and recycle iPhones. These companies are working to make their supply chains more sustainable and reduce their environmental impact.
