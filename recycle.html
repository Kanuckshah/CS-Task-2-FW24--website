Reduce Your Impact

  • Buy energy-efficient devices: Look for ENERGY STAR-certified computers and devices to lower your energy consumption.
  • Extend the life of your electronics: Instead of upgrading to the latest model, try upgrading components like memory or storage in your existing computer.
  • Use cloud services: Cloud computing can reduce the need for physical hardware and lower your overall environmental footprint.

Recycle E-Waste

  • Certified recyclers: Always take your old electronics to certified e-waste recyclers. They have the technology to safely dismantle devices and recover valuable materials like gold and copper, while properly disposing of hazardous substances.
  • Companies offering recycling programs: Major companies like Dell, Best Buy, and Staples offer e-waste recycling services. Some even offer incentives, like store credit, for recycling your old devices.

How E-Waste is Recycled

  • Dismantling: Recyclers take apart computers and separate valuable materials from hazardous components.
  • Shredding: Devices are shredded and sorted to recover materials like plastics, metals, and glass.
  • Refining: Precious metals like gold and copper are extracted for reuse in new electronics.
